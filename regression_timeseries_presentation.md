## 1. Background to the Study

Academic performance is a key indicator of students' success and institutional effectiveness in higher education. It reflects how well students understand course content and meet academic requirements over time. In tertiary institutions, students' academic performance is commonly measured using the Grade Point Average (GPA) or Cumulative Grade Point Average (CGPA), which often varies across semesters due to academic workload, study habits, attendance, and prior academic achievement.

In recent years, there has been increasing interest in the application of mathematical and statistical techniques to analyze and predict students' academic performance. Applied mathematics provides tools such as regression analysis, time-series modelling, and stochastic processes that can be used to:

- Describe performance trends
- Identify influential factors
- Forecast future outcomes

These models are valuable for academic planning, early identification of at-risk students, and informed decision-making by educational administrators.

---

## 2. Problem Statement

Students' academic performance in higher education often varies from one semester to another due to differences in academic workload, study habits, attendance, and prior academic achievements. However, most institutions rely on raw data without systematically analysing these factors or predicting future performance. This lack of quantitative insight makes it hard to identify students who are not performing well and to take quick actions.

---

## 3. Aim & Objectives

### Aim

The principal goal of this research is to apply mathematical and statistical modelling techniques to academic performance data in order to:

- Identify trends
- Quantify influencing factors
- Predict students' academic outcomes for improved decision-making

### Objectives

1. Collect and organize students' academic performance data including GPA, attendance rate, study hours, and course load.
2. Analyse patterns in students' academic performance and identify patterns of improvement or decline.
3. Develop an applied mathematical and statistical model that explains the relationship between academic performance and its influencing factors.

---

## 4. Scope & Limitations

### Scope

- Focuses on modelling and predicting students' academic performance using GPA data
- Considers quantitative academic variables including:
  - Students' Grade Point Average (GPA)
  - Previous academic performance
  - Attendance rate
  - Study hours
  - Number of registered courses

### Limitations

- Some variables (such as study hours and attendance) may be estimated or self-reported, which can introduce bias or measurement errors.
- The regression models assume linear relationships between variables, whereas real academic performance may involve nonlinear or complex interactions.
- Limited generalizability, since the study is based on data from a specific department or institution.
- Restricted set of variables, as only academic and study-related factors were considered.

## 8. Research Gaps & Solutions

1. **Lack of Rigorous Mathematical Models** — Most institutions rely on descriptive summaries of GPA without applying rigorous mathematical models.
2. **Lack of Predictive Tools for Early Identification of At-Risk Students** — No systematic mechanism to flag struggling students early.
3. **Inadequate Analysis of Performance Trends Over Time** — Performance is reviewed in isolation rather than as a time series.
4. **Weak Integration of Theory and Practical Application** — Some studies focus on theory without practical deployment.
